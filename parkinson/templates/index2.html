Understanding Parkinson's Disease

A progressive neurodegenerative disorder affecting motor control

What is it?

Parkinson's disease is characterized by the progressive degeneration of dopamine-producing neurons in the substantia nigra of the brain, primarily affecting motor control.

Main Symptoms

  • Tremors at rest
  • Muscle rigidity
  • Bradykinesia (slowness)
  • Balance disorders
  • Voice alterations

Epidemiology

Second most common neurodegenerative disease after Alzheimer's, affecting over 10 million people worldwide.

UCI Parkinson Dataset

Comprehensive Vocal Analysis Database

Our model utilizes the reference UCI Parkinson's Disease Classification dataset, a standardized collection of vocal measurements widely used in scientific research for Parkinson's detection.

Our Methodology

Scientific analysis based on validated acoustic data

Our Mission

As data science and AI engineering students, we've developed an innovative system for early detection of Parkinson's disease through voice analysis.

Technical Approach

We trained several machine learning models on the UCI Parkinson's dataset, containing 22 vocal features from 195 individuals.

Tested Models
  • Logistic Regression
  • K-Nearest Neighbors
  • Random Forest Classifier
  • Multi-layer Perceptron
  • Support Vector Machine
  • Stacking Ensemble

Results Obtained

Best model performance:
97% accuracy
Analyzed Features
  • Fundamental frequency
  • Jitter (variations)
  • Shimmer (amplitude)
  • HNR (harmonic-to-noise ratio)
  • RPDE (entropy)
  • DFA (fluctuation analysis)
  • PPE (period entropy)
Key Advantage: The stacking ensemble approach combines the strengths of multiple models, achieving superior predictive accuracy compared to any single algorithm alone.
